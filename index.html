I'm a PhD student at the University of São Paulo in Brazil, the same place where I graduated in astronomy. My research is focused on observational analysis of the Milky Way's disk, from its formation to the smaller substructures of the outer disk. Some of these substructures are produced by the interaction of the disk with accreting dwarf galaxies, which is also the subject of research by my group in Brazil. The chemistry and dynamics of the Milky Way's stars can tell us details about our Galaxy's turbulent past. My main interest is to help solve the puzzle of the history of the Milky Way. You can check the details about my research and interests on the research page and my results can be found on the publications page.

I'm also an enthusiast of projects related to teaching astronomy and scientific dissemination, especially for children. You can find out more details about each of them on the outreach page. Talking to people and seeing their enthusiasm makes me even more excited about my work.
